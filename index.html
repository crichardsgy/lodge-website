Our History


The Karanambu (car-a-nam-bo) was once a working cattle ranch and balata collection station, when it was first settled in by Edward “tiny” McTurk and his wife Connie, in 1927. Today, it’s as an ecotourism destination by the name of “Karanambu lodge”. The lodge was established by Edward and Connie’s daughter, Diane McTurk and was best known for having been her home Sadly, Diane died in 2016 after dedicating her life to the rehabilitation of the giant river otter. However, her work lives on through the Karanambu trust. he Karanambu trust is an organization that was set up By the McTurk family in 1997 to help fulfill diane’s vision of establishing karanambu’s lands as a protected area. Diane’s fabled hospitality continues endure at karanambu. In keeping with ‘aunty di’s’ legacy, we welcome travelers, adventurers, naturalists, film makers, conservationists and really anyone who loves nature and wants to experience the beauty that is found in our part of the untamed Rupununi.
